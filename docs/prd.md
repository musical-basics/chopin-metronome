# Product Requirements Document: The Chopin Polyrhythm Metronome

## 1. Product Overview
* **Product Name:** PolyRhythm Pro (or "Chopin's Nightmare")
* **Core Philosophy:** Standard 4/4 metronomes are for pop musicians. Romantic-era classical pianists need to practice absurd mathematical ratios like 22-against-7 without their brains melting.
* **Primary Goal:** A highly precise, visually intuitive web-based metronome designed exclusively for playing two conflicting time signatures simultaneously.

## 2. Core Experience & Features
* **Dual Pulse Engine:** Two completely independent metronome engines running simultaneously (Left Hand vs. Right Hand).
* **Ratio Selectors:** Two number inputs where the user defines the polyrhythm (e.g., Left = 3, Right = 4). Allows up to ridiculous numbers like 22 and 7 (looking at you, Nocturne Op. 9 No. 1).
* **BPM Control:** A master BPM slider and input field.
* **Distinct Audio Cues:** * Left Hand: A low-pitched, resonant "tock" (e.g., a low woodblock).
  * Right Hand: A high-pitched, sharp "tick" (e.g., a high woodblock or rimclick).
  * The "One": An accented chime when both hands hit beat 1 at the exact same time.
* **Visual Phasing:** Two pulsing visual indicators (circles or bars) that flash exactly when the audio ticks, giving a visual representation of how the rhythms interlock.

## 3. Design Aesthetic
* **Vibe:** Sleek, acoustic luxury. Dark mode background (`#0A0A0A`), but using gold/brass accents (`#D4A853`) to match the aesthetic of a Steinway grand piano or the DreamPlay brand.
